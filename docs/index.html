Overview

This portal accompanies the working paper "Unlocking Economic Freedom". It applies Hidalgo & Hausmann's economic complexity methodology to institutional indicators (Fraser EFW, 2023) to produce a data-driven hierarchy of indicators by complexity, a network space of institutional similarities, and a country-level framework for reform prioritization.

Methodology — short version

1. Data

Fraser EFW 2025 Annual Report, 2023 cross-section: 165 countries × 45 leaf indicators (pillar aggregates excluded to avoid double counting).

2. Binary specialization matrix M

For each indicator, the distribution across countries is classified by Jenks Natural Breaks with n=3 classes. A country specializes (M[c,i]=1) if its raw score exceeds the lower bound of the top class. Jenks adapts to each indicator's distribution; fixed thresholds (e.g. ≥ 7) misbehave because the maxima of some indicators are below 7.

3. Indicator Complexity Index (ICI)

Eigenvalue method on the doubly-normalized matrix M^pp' = M^T (M / diversity) / ubiquity: the second-largest eigenvector becomes the raw ICI. Sign is fixed so high ubiquity maps to low ICI. Paper's variant: shift to non-negative and rescale so weights sum to 100.

4. Country Complexity Index (CCI)

CCI[c] = sum_i M[c,i] × ICI[i]. With ICI summing to 100, CCI is bounded [0, 100] and reads as "% of maximum complexity captured". Departure from classical Hidalgo (z-scored eigenvector): unbounded broad baskets are not penalized with negative ICI weights.

5. Proximity matrix φ

φ[i,j] = #(countries with both i and j) / max(ubiquity[i], ubiquity[j]). Symmetric; max-normalization is more conservative than the min variant used by some authors.

6. Indicator clustering (Table 4)

Indicators with max φ < 0.50 form a basket. On the remaining core, a MST + P90 graph is built; shortest-path distances feed into Ward k=3 clustering. The smallest cluster merges with the basket as residual. The two larger clusters are split via Ward k=2 into A/B halves (A = higher mean ICI). Final labels 1A/1B/2A/2B/3 are assigned by descending mean ICI.

7. Density (probability of progress)

density[c,i] = sum_{j≠i} φ[i,j] × M[c,j] / sum_{j≠i} φ[i,j]. Diagonal of φ is excluded so an indicator never counts as its own neighbor.

8. Reform priority score

For each (country, indicator) pair where the country is not yet specialized: score = ICI × density × frac_of_threshold, where frac_of_threshold = raw_score / Jenks_threshold. Higher score = simultaneously high complexity, structural reachability, and current closeness to specialization.
